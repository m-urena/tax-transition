BISON WEALTH MANAGEMENT
Portfolio Transition Optimizer — Compliance Review
Date: April 23, 2026
================================================================================


WHAT IS MATHEMATICALLY CORRECT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

  - LP objective: maximize proceeds subject to net tax <= budget
  - Tax per lot: gain x rate x fraction_sold
  - Loss positions get negative tax coefficients; optimizer harvests them first
  - Minimum Tax scenario (budget = $0) correctly forces sells only on
    loss positions
  - Max Transition budget = sum of all potential tax if every gain position
    were sold in full
  - Sell bounds correctly prevent selling overlapping positions below their
    target model weight


ITEMS COMPLIANCE MAY FLAG
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. CROSS-TYPE LOSS NETTING (real math gap)

   The optimizer multiplies each position by its own tax rate:
   gain(i) x rate(i) x fraction_sold(i). IRS rules require netting
   long-term and short-term gains/losses separately, then offsetting
   if they are opposite signs.

   Example: sell $10,000 ST gain (37% rate) and $5,000 LT loss (20% rate).

     Tool calculates:  $10,000 x 0.37 - $5,000 x 0.20 = $2,700
     IRS correct:      LT loss offsets $5,000 of ST gain
                       Net ST taxable = $5,000 x 0.37  = $1,850

   The tool overstates tax in cross-type offset scenarios. This is a planning
   approximation that most transition tools make, but compliance should be
   aware of it.


2. NET INVESTMENT INCOME TAX (NIIT) NOT INCLUDED

   High-income clients (MAGI > $200,000 single / $250,000 married filing
   jointly) owe an additional 3.8% on net investment income, which includes
   capital gains. The tool has no field for NIIT. Advisors must manually
   account for it by increasing the LT rate input (e.g., enter 23.8%
   instead of 20%). The current disclaimer does not mention this.


3. STATE AND LOCAL TAXES NOT INCLUDED

   The tool uses only the federal rates entered in the sidebar. State capital
   gains taxes can be significant:

     California:   13.3%
     New York:     10.9%
     New Jersey:    5.7%
     (and others)

   The current disclaimer does not call this out explicitly.


4. WASH SALE RULE — NO WARNING DISPLAYED

   The optimizer's structure largely avoids creating wash sales for positions
   that appear in both the current portfolio and the target model, because:
   (a) it will not sell those positions below their target weight, and
   (b) buys only fill positions that are underweight after sells.

   However, the UI displays no wash sale warning. An advisor placing trades
   outside this tool, or buying a harvested-loss position within 30 days,
   could trigger wash sale disallowance. A disclosure should be added.


5. FRACTIONAL SHARES IN TRADE TABLE

   The optimizer works in continuous fractions. The trade export shows
   "Shares Sold" rounded to two decimal places (e.g., 73.42 shares), which
   is not executable in most custodian systems. The export should note that
   share quantities must be rounded to whole shares before placing orders.


6. TIMELINE ASSUMES STATIC PRICES

   The multi-year transition timeline holds all security prices constant at
   today's values. In practice, unrealized gains grow as prices rise, meaning
   the actual transition will likely take longer and incur more tax than the
   tool projects. The timeline chart has no disclaimer about this assumption.


RECOMMENDED DISCLAIMER TEXT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Current footer reads:
  "Illustrative purposes only. Tax estimates assume LT rate X%, ST rate X%.
   Actual liability may differ. Consult a qualified tax advisor before
   transacting."

Suggested replacement:
  "Illustrative purposes only. Tax estimates use the entered federal LT/ST
   rates and do not reflect: state or local taxes, the 3.8% Net Investment
   Income Tax (NIIT), IRS capital loss netting and ordering rules, wash sale
   restrictions, or future changes in security prices or tax law. Share
   quantities shown are unrounded and must be adjusted to whole shares before
   execution. Consult a qualified tax advisor before transacting."
